## Verbatim Transcription

Dear [Neurologist C],

I would be grateful if you could kindly assess this patient who presents with a complex constellation of neurological and systemic symptoms, including features raising the possibility of parkinsonism, although the overall clinical picture remains atypical.

I met him once during a video call from Portugal where he was with his family. I understand he is now back in London

This patient has a background of longstanding hypotension with prior syncopal episodes, and Bipolar Affective Disorder (diagnosed 2018). In 2022, while taking lamotrigine and quetiapine, they developed symptoms suggestive of parkinsonism, including discoordinated movements, memory impairment, and twitching. These symptoms resolved following a switch to lithium, suggesting a possible medication-related phenomenon at that time.

However, since 2024, the patient reports a recurrence of concerning symptoms, including:

- Reduced exercise tolerance (previously able to walk approximately one mile daily)
- Significant fatigue, particularly post-exertional
- Episodes of palpitations and chest pain
- Intermittent worsening of symptoms after meals
- Bradycardia during sleep
- A prior fainting episode following COVID vaccination in 2021

There is no clear orthostatic intolerance, and notably, the patient reports improvement in symptoms in warmer climates (e.g. during a recent trip to Brazil).

Additional background includes possible post-vaccine ME/CFS-like illness and suspected dysautonomia. There is also a question of lithium-related diabetes insipidus, with reported low plasma osmolality and high fluid turnover (e.g. ~3 L intake with ~3.7 L urine output), with some symptomatic improvement following adjustment of fluid and salt intake.

Investigations to date include:

- CT pulmonary angiogram (2021) – no pulmonary embolism (despite elevated D-dimer)
- CT and MRI brain (with spectroscopy) – showing gliosis without specific metabolic abnormality
- Cardiac investigations (details unclear; further clarification pending)

Current medications include lithium, electrolyte supplementation, magnesium, vitamin D, vitamin B12, coenzyme Q10, omega-3, calcium, and a high salt intake (~6 g daily). The patient does not smoke or consume alcohol.

While dysautonomia remains a possible unifying diagnosis, the history of prior parkinsonian features, together with ongoing fatigue, discoordination, and cognitive symptoms, raises concern about a possible underlying or evolving neurological disorder that would benefit from specialist assessment. He recently had another episode of dystonia (he shared with me videos which I could not review), this sounds very atypical of ME/dysautonomia.

I would be very grateful for your expert opinion regarding:

- Whether these symptoms may represent a form of parkinsonism or other movement disorder
- The relevance of prior medication-induced symptoms
- Any further neurological investigations that may be indicated

Please do not hesitate to contact me if further information would be helpful.

Yours sincerely

[Cardiologist A (London)]
Consultant Cardiologist
