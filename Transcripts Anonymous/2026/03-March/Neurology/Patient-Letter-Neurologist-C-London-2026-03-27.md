**Summary and Management Plan:**

The clinical picture, with profound fatigue and intermittent, fluctuating abnormal movements, is complex. Your neurological examination today is largely reassuring.

The abnormal movements you are experiencing are not typical for a drug-induced movement disorder, given their paroxysmal and fluctuating nature. The overall presentation is most consistent with a functional neurological disorder (FND). This is a condition where the 'software' of the nervous system is not working correctly, despite the 'hardware' being structurally intact. FND often occurs in the context of other stressors, such as your high-pressure job and caring responsibilities, and underlying conditions like bipolar disorder. The fatigue is likely multifactorial, with contributions from the FND and your underlying psychiatric condition. The cauda equina-like episode is also likely part of the FND, given that imaging has not shown a structural cause and the symptoms have resolved.

At this time, I do not recommend any new medications for the movement disorder. I have suggested potentially testing for an acetylcholine receptor antibody blood test to rule out myasthenia gravis, which is an uncommon cause of fluctuating weakness, although I anticipate this will be negative as you have no clinical signs of the condition.

I advised you to continue with your current management under your psychiatrist and renal physician. The most effective treatment for FND is often a multi-disciplinary approach, primarily involving management of the underlying psychiatric condition through talking therapies and appropriate medication, alongside developing cognitive strategies to manage the functional symptoms. It is a clinical diagnosis based on the pattern of symptoms and signs, as there is no specific test for it.

The benefit of this plan is to avoid the risks of unnecessary investigations while acknowledging the risk, albeit low, that a subtle underlying organic cause could be missed. An alternative would be to pursue more aggressive testing immediately, but this is not felt to be clinically indicated given the extensive negative workup to date.
