21 September 2020

Clinic Date: 18 September 2020

Dear Dr

Re: Mr Patient [SURNAME REMOVED] - dob [DOB REMOVED]
[ADDRESS REMOVED]

I had the pleasure to talk to Patient again today over the phone for a follow up appointment and as part of my outpatients' clinic at [Clinic A (London)].

At the time of our appointment Patient was taking Quetiapine XL 300mg, nocte and Lamictal at the variable dose of 250-300mg, mane. This with good effect.

He does report 6 months of well-being with overall stable mood, energy levels and functioning. He has been steady and consistent with his rhythms at work, and continues to be in a relationship with Brazilian woman that has now joined him to live together in Portugal. Describes his mood as stable and calm however has registered a bit of a drop in the month of June which has prompted him to reduce the dose of Lamictal from 300mg, mane to 250mg, mane and more recently in the month of August he reports experiencing a couple hours of high with increased speech and elated mood towards the end of the day each day. He recalls that taking Quetiapine XL 300mg, nocte did curb it down. This mood change prompted him to increase the Lamictal back again to 300mg, mane.

He believes is happy with current combination of mood. I have advised him to slightly increase Quetiapine of 50mg max 100mg, for a total of 400mg, nocte should he experience another episode of elation and leave the dose of Lamictal unchanged or engage in a slow and careful increase of Lamictal up to 400mg, OD (normally Lamictal tends to act more slowly than Quetiapine).

He reports having felt particularly low at the beg of this week but this is likely to be due to COVID infection which appears to be mild. He is experiencing some of the symptoms- fever, muscle aches and headaches – but only managed to get a test privately so unclear whether this is a COVID-related infection. He has been advised to self-isolate for 10 days which he will. He is aware of following steps should his condition deteriorate.

He is planning to go back to Portugal as he finds it more conducive to his recovery and stability also of his mental health and now that he can work remotely does not find any reason to be in London.

I was not able to complete a full assessment of his mental state over the phone; however, he sounded pleasant and engaging as usual. Reactive in affect and in good spirit with no delusional beliefs and no abnormalities of perception reported or detected. I did not detect any risk to self or others during today's conversation.

I congratulated Patient for the good work done and progress made. I believe that he is now in full recovery however he will need to continue current medications.

I confirmed current combination of medication at current dose and suggested for him to be followed up by a psychiatrist in his local area which he says he will.

I now discharge Patient from my caseload wishing him the very best for his future.

Yours sincerely

[Psychiatrist A (London)]
Consultant Psychiatrist
