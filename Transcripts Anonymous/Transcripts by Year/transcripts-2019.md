# GP Letter — Psychiatrist A (London) — 08-03-2019

## Verbatim Transcription

8 March 2019

Clinic Date: 8 March 2019

Dear Doctor

Re: Mr Patient [SURNAME REMOVED] - dob [DOB REMOVED]
[ADDRESS REMOVED]

I had the pleasure to meet today during my outpatients clinic at [Clinic A (London)] Mr Patient [SURNAME REMOVED].

At the time of our first meeting, Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] was taking Abilify 10mg, mane and Promethazine 50mg, nocte for a manic relapse of his bipolar disorder.

Today he comes to the clinic as afflicted by restlessness and asking for alternatives to current medications.

Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] recalls an hypomanic relapse in January 2019 when he started feeling easily irritated by people around him who he would find incredibly slow and would trigger violent thoughts about beating them up or punish them for being so slow. He also experienced racing thoughts, fluctuations in his mood with sudden changes from elation to low mood, increased sexual libido and overspending.

He asked to be reviewed by the public sector by was denied any urgent appointment. He then attended A&E and was given Abilify 10mg, mane but this gave him restlessness and increased appetite and was unable to work and carry on his normal life feeling so restless. He was recently taken on by home treatment team but has been discharged following a three days trip to Cologne to meet a woman he fell in love with.

He feels that Abilify has helped him and he can manage sleep with the Promethazine but it seems that he requires additional medications to aid with sleep, the racing thoughts have slightly dissipated and the violent thoughts have reduced; however, mood continues to fluctuate rapidly and still feel hypersexual and with a tendency to overspending. He specifies the sex is promiscuous but protected with consensual female adult partners met through phone apps. He denies any grandiose beliefs of delusional nature or any other psychotic symptom.

He is currently followed up by mental health services in [Borough] and [GP B (London)] in primary care. He was told that he had an appointment with the community mental health team on 18/03 but realised that he could not wait because of rapid deterioration of mental state.

**Past Psychiatric History**

Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] recalls a first manic episode in Feb 2018 following sentimental involvement with a woman. He thought he could see 'love' and thought he needed to start a new religion, he stopped sleeping, thought he was becoming a genius, started becoming hyper-religious going to Buddhist centre every day and then felt a 'detonation' after which he could see 'love', he could sense 'vibrations' coming out of living beings. After a while he began feeling unsafe with pressured speech and racing thoughts and started flooding the woman he was in love with text messages until she eventually recoiled and blocked him. Denies any auditory hallucination, thought broadcasting or insertion at the time.

He then recalls reading the symptoms of psychosis on the internet and recognising some of the features. He went to the GP who gave him sertraline. This triggered a rapid cycling episode.

He was eventually rescued by his father who took him back to Portugal where he was admitted to hospital for over a month and treated with Abilify first and Olanzapine later (dose unclear) together with Sodium Valproate at a relative low dose, 200mg or 400 mg (dose unclear).

He later had to stop Sodium Valproate because made him to drowsy. He also had to stop Abilify after a month because of erectile dysfunction. He reports undergoing a period of depression when he spent a month in bed. He later started to recuperate going to the gym, he found a new job and slowly started feeling better. He continued Sodium Valproate 200mg, in divided doses until July 2018 and eventually stopped in August because he felt low and couldn't concentrate at work.

In January 2019, when he started experiencing the first manic symptoms he went to the GP and was given Sodium Valproate 200mg, in divided dose but had bad digestive problem with diarrhoea alternated to constipation, He asked for a referral to the public sector but was told that he could be seen only in two months. Then he went to A&E and hospitalised for one night and recommended to start Abilify 10 mg, mane and to stop Sodium valproate in view of the digestive collateral effects.

**Medical history**
Nil of note. No allergies.

**Family History**
Father and mother in their 60ies. Mother possibly affected by dementia.
Younger brother tried to commit suicide at 11 ys. old. Eldest brother suffered from a depressive episode.

**Past Psychiatric History**
Patient describes himself with a cyclothymic temperament. Saw a psychologist early on as had a difficult upbringing. Works as a video-editor in London. Lives in shared household. Has a good support network of friends aware of his bipolar illness.

**Drugs & Alcohol History**
Has taken recreational drugs like MDMA, hashish and marijuana in the past. Has not touched drugs in the past 6 months as aware of negative impact on his mental state. Has not used alcohol in the past 3-4 weeks for same reasons.

**Forensic History**
Nil of note.

**Mental State Examination**
Young and slim Caucasian male well groomed, slightly overfamiliar. Visibly agitated with increased psychomotor agitation, standing up and pacing around. Speech increased in rate. Mood easily changeable, irritable at times. His thoughts are not always congruent and goal directed and present with derailment and tangentiallity. Denies abnormality of perception or any current delusional beliefs. No current risks to self or others detected during this meeting. Only risk is of further deterioration if left untreated.

**Formulation**
Manic relapse in bipolar disorder. We discussed different treatment options in terms of antipsychotic and mood stabilisers medications. He has preferred Quetiapine after hearing side effects profile.

**Plan**
I recommended to start Quetiapine XL 300mg, tonight to be increased to 600mg, nocte starting tmw night and to meet soon next week Wednesday 13/03/19 at 20.00.

## Verbatim Transcription

13 March 2019

Clinic Date: 13 March 2019

Dear Doctor

Re: Mr Patient [SURNAME REMOVED] - dob [DOB REMOVED]
[ADDRESS REMOVED]

I had the pleasure to meet again Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] today for a follow up appointment during my outpatients' clinic at [Clinic A (London)].

Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] was taking Quetiapine XL 600mg, nocte for a manic relapse of a bipolar disorder as per clinic letter dated 08/03/19.

Today Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] reports great improvement of his mental state and states feeling fine and stable 'very normal'. He is able to sleep a lot better without the aid of Promethazine for 8 hours per night. Also his mood has greatly normalised without any feature of irritability and without harbouring violent thoughts for the less energetic and able ones. Sexual function and libido have also reduced to baseline and he is now able to function and concentrate at work, he does not experience racing thoughts any longer. He has recently gone back to work as free-lance and he is now teaching in Brighton for few days a week. He complains of milder fluctuations of his mood however these are much more bearable than previously. His current main fear is a depressive episode which he knows it normally follows mania.

Today Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] presents much more composed and appropriate with normal levels of engagement and normal speech. Significantly calmer with no psychomotor agitation or restlessness. Mood is rated subjectively as within his normal usual range and objectively noted as slightly subdued. No delusional beliefs or abnormalities of perception are reported and no indirect signs are detected. Cognition is normal with excellent insight into his mental health needs and the need to take medications. There are no risks to self or others detected during today's meeting.

We discussed the opportunity to start a mood stabiliser as this will need to be his long term treatment strategy since it is opportune to withdraw antipsychotic medication after 4 weeks from complete resolution of psychotic symptoms as per BAP and Maudsley Guidelines. This to reduce the risk of onset of depressive episodes. Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] expresses a preference for Lamictal as mood stabiliser for its benign side effect profile.

I recommended to continue taking Quetiapine XL 600mg, nocte for the time being and to start Lamictal 25 mg, mane for 14 days and to increment 25mg, every 14 days until it reaches 100mg, mane as per BNF titration.

I would be very grateful if you could start repeat prescription for Quetiapine XL 600mg, nocte and Lamictal 25mg, mane for 14 days and if you could continue to titrate Lamictal according to BNF-that is with an increment of 25 mg every 14 days until it reaches 100mg, mane.

Should you have any queries please do not hesitate to ask.

Yours sincerely

[Psychiatrist A (London)]
Consultant Psychiatrist

6 November 2019

Clinic Date: 6 November 2019

Dear Doctor

Re: Mr Patient [SURNAME REMOVED] - dob [DOB REMOVED]
[ADDRESS REMOVED]

I had the pleasure to meet again Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] today for a follow up appointment during my outpatients' clinic at [Clinic A (London)].

Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] is taking Quetiapine XL 300mg, nocte and Lamictal 150 mg, mane.

Patient comes back to the clinic asking for his medications to be changed to Seroquel and Lamictal instead of the generic brand. He states that he has been unstable since the last time we met in may and is unhappy about the public sector. He tells me that Seroquel was changed to immediate release and that formulation knocked him out and was unable to wake up the next day. He reports that mood has been unstable feeling more easily irritated, nervous, impatient or quick tempered. He also easily plunged into depression with poor motivation, depressed mood and frequent headaches.

He denies any angry outburst, any frank manic or psychotic symptoms.

Today he presents his usual pleasant and talking individual. His speech was normal for rate and volume. He has reactive affect and normal mood. No manic or psychotic symptoms were reported nor indirect signs were noted. He denies any thought/intention or plan to harm self or others.

We discussed how unlikely it may be that current variation in his mood and energy levels may due to the changes of the brand. I recommended him trying to transition on high dose of Lamotrigine/Lamictal or eventually trying Lithium if higher dose of Quetiapine were poorly tolerated.

For the time being her only asked for Seroquel and Lamictal to be prescribed and to evaluate at a later stage whether it was opportune to make any further change to his treatment plan.

I prescribed Patient with Seroquel XL 300mg, nocte (10 tabs) and Lamictal 150 mg, mane (10 tabs).

I would be grateful if you could continue repeat prescription for same meds: Seroquel XL 300mg, nocte and Lamictal 150 mg, mane.

Yours sincerely

[Psychiatrist A (London)]
Consultant Psychiatrist
