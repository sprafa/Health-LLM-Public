He tells me that his life and mood are very stable. He continues to live in Porto where he work in postproduction for the film industry. At the time of the call he was attending a short course on screenwriting at well renowned film school outside of London. He met a new partner in Porto and does not think that he will relocate to London any time soon. He is facilitated by his job that can be done remotely.

However, he also tells me that he is getting concerned about his physical health. During Christmas he attended A&E for an episode of hyperglycaemia. He does admit that he ate too many sweets but does not think that it was excessive.

He completed HbA1c which he was told is bordering pre-diabetic levels. He also eliminated all the carbs and sugars in his diet and engaged in regular physical exercise but the glucose levels remain high. He believes that Seroquel may have contributed to glucose dysregulation.

Today [SURNAME REMOVED] presented very well, always jovial and intelligent. Smiling and bright with normal speech, coherent thinking and no manic or psychotic symptoms reported and none elicited. Cognition was intact. His insight is always very good and I did not detect any risk during today's consultation.

I did confirm that Seroquel can have an impact on metabolism and metabolic measure. It is rare but possible.

I recommended to complete a screening for diabetic and pre-diabetic condition and be supported by a specialist where he lives.

He also needs to consider, in conjunction with his psychiatrist and endocrinologist, whether to change current treatment plan. This will need to be carefully considered and weighted against risks of developing a frank diabetic condition and/ or a relapse of primary mental health condition, namely bipolar disorder.

I have known Patient for few years and he has in fact benefitted greatly from current treatment plan. Initially the idea was to transition to taking solely Lamictal but he never found comfortable in reducing Seroquel too much.

If it is considered necessary to change current treatment plan I would suggest the following options in the following order:

1) Cross-titrate current regime to Lamotrigine only at high dose -up to 400mg, daily as per BNF. Lamotrigine is an excellent mood stabiliser with no impact on metabolism or weight;
2) Alternatively, Patient can try a combination of Lamictal and Aripiprazole. I know that Patient tried Aripiprazole before without liking it too much but it was for a short period. I would encourage him to trial aripiprazole for a longer period before ruling it out. Please bear in mind that aripiprazole can also work as monotherapy.
3) If aripiprazole in combination or as monotherapy does not work, I would suggest trying Sodium Valproate in monotherapy or in combination with Lamictal. Sodium valproate is an excellent mood stabiliser. It may have an impact on metabolism and weight as well but less than quetiapine.
4) If all the above fails, I would suggest trying Lithium who is the gold standard for the treatment of bipolar disorder however comes with negative impact on kidney and thyroid in the longer term.

I told Patient that I was not able to provide him with support needed for this type of task and he needs an higher level of input than what can be given remotely as he would require frequent follow ups and also an understanding of the services available in his local area. I recommended Patient to find a psychiatrist he trusts in Porto that can support him whilst building together a treatment plan that Patient finds suitable without negative side effect on his physical health.

I now discharge him from my caseload wishing him the very best for his future.

Yours sincerely
