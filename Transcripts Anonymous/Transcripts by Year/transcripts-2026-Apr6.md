4. **Possible Lithium-Related Diabetes Insipidus**

- Lithium
- Two electrolyte packets daily
- Magnesium
- Vitamin D
- Vitamin B12
- Coenzyme Q10
- Omega-3
- Calcium
- Approximately 6 g of salt daily

**You reported the following symptoms:**
- Fainting episode following vaccination in 2021
- Bradycardia during sleep
- Reduced exercise tolerance (previously able to walk approximately one mile daily)
- Significant fatigue, especially after activity
- Episodes of palpitations and chest pain
- Symptoms sometimes worsen after eating
- No clear orthostatic intolerance
- No heat intolerance; in fact, you felt significantly better during a recent trip to Brazil

You also reported laboratory tests suggesting **low plasma osmolality**. After discussing your symptoms with your psychiatrist and reviewing information independently, you increased your salt and fluid intake, which initially improved your symptoms. More recently, reducing water intake appears to have led to further improvement. For example, on a recent day you reported **fluid intake of approximately 3 L with urine output of about 3.7 L**.

1. **Cardiac investigations** — It would be helpful to clarify exactly what tests were performed. In particular, I would like to know whether you have had: Troponin or NT-proBNP testing, an ECG, a recent Holter monitor. 2. **CT scan of the chest**, possibly suggesting a clot in the neck.

3. **MRI of the brain with spectroscopy**, which confirmed gliosis but did not reveal any specific metabolic abnormalities.

**Clinical Impression**

At this stage, my impression is that you may be experiencing **dysautonomia**, potentially related to prior COVID infection or vaccination.

There may also be an element of **diabetes insipidus**, which could be related to lithium therapy even at relatively low doses. You appear to be particularly sensitive to medications, which is important to consider when planning treatment.

For this reason, I would prefer to minimise the introduction of additional medications wherever possible.

**Recommended Further Evaluation**

1. **Autoimmune screening blood tests**, if these have not been done recently.

2. **Updated cardiovascular assessment**, including:
   - ECG
   - 24-hour blood pressure monitoring
   - Holter monitor
   This would help rule out any potential cardiac effects of lithium.

3. **Assessment of possible diabetes insipidus**, including:
   - Plasma osmolality
   - Serum sodium
   - Urine osmolality

**Possible Treatment Considerations**

Depending on the results of the above assessments and your blood pressure readings, medications such as **fludrocortisone** or **midodrine** could potentially be considered to help:
- Maintain slightly higher blood pressure
- Improve sodium balance

However, this should only be considered after further evaluation.

**Lifestyle Recommendations**

I would encourage you to:
- Gradually reintroduce **gentle exercise**, such as short walks or yoga.
- Try to **leave the house at least once daily** where possible.
- Maintain **regular social interaction with family and friends**, as face-to-face contact can play an important role in wellbeing during prolonged illness.

Please feel free to contact me once you have the results of the recommended investigations. I will be happy to review them and help guide the next steps in your management.
