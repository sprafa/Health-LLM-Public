Clinic Date: 13 March 2019

Dear Doctor

Re: Mr Patient [SURNAME REMOVED] - dob [DOB REMOVED]

I had the pleasure to meet again Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] today for a follow up appointment during my outpatients' clinic at [Clinic A (London)].

Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] was taking Quetiapine XL 600mg, nocte for a manic relapse of a bipolar disorder as per clinic letter dated 08/03/19.

Today Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] reports great improvement of his mental state and states feeling fine and stable 'very normal'. He is able to sleep a lot better without the aid of Promethazine for 8 hours per night. Also his mood has greatly normalised without any feature of irritability and without harbouring violent thoughts for the less energetic and able ones. Sexual function and libido have also reduced to baseline and he is now able to function and concentrate at work, he does not experience racing thoughts any longer. He has recently gone back to work as free-lance and he is now teaching in Brighton for few days a week. He complains of milder fluctuations of his mood however these are much more bearable than previously. His current main fear is a depressive episode which he knows it normally follows mania.

Today Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] presents much more composed and appropriate with normal levels of engagement and normal speech. Significantly calmer with no psychomotor agitation or restlessness. Mood is rated subjectively as within his normal usual range and objectively noted as slightly subdued. No delusional beliefs or abnormalities of perception are reported and no indirect signs are detected. Cognition is normal with excellent insight into his mental health needs and the need to take medications. There are no risks to self or others detected during today's meeting.

We discussed the opportunity to start a mood stabiliser as this will need to be his long term treatment strategy since it is opportune to withdraw antipsychotic medication after 4 weeks from complete resolution of psychotic symptoms as per BAP and Maudsley Guidelines. This to reduce the risk of onset of depressive episodes. Mr [SURNAME REMOVED] expresses a preference for Lamictal as mood stabiliser for its benign side effect profile.

I recommended to continue taking Quetiapine XL 600mg, nocte for the time being and to start Lamictal 25 mg, mane for 14 days and to increment 25mg, every 14 days until it reaches 100mg, mane as per BNF titration.

I would be very grateful if you could start repeat prescription for Quetiapine XL 600mg, nocte and Lamictal 25mg, mane for 14 days and if you could continue to titrate Lamictal according to BNF-that is with an increment of 25 mg every 14 days until it reaches 100mg, mane.

Should you have any queries please do not hesitate to ask.

Yours sincerely

[Psychiatrist A (London)]
Consultant Psychiatrist
