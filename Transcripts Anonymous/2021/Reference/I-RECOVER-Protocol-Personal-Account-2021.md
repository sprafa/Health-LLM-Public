## Verbatim Transcription

YSK about the I-RECOVER protocol from the FLCCC and Mast Cell Activation - my side effects seem improve after I had some over the counter anti histamines and regular supplements that are indicated for long haul covid

Hey fellas,
I've been posting for a while just wanted to share my experience. 32M, had 1 month of serious symptoms, mostly cleared up with anti histamines and supplement diet. Johnson vacc.

**TLDR - there's a list at the end with my supplement diet.**

Symptoms
Day 1-3 normal headaches, went away. By the end of day 3 I felt ready to come back to work.
BUT THEN
Day 4 came up. No feeling on the entire right side of my body. Hospital, CT scan. All clean. Slowly recover.
Day 5 came up. I can't lift my right arm and feel enormous weakness on right leg. Hospital, CT Scan again! I get muscle spams throughout my body and I think I'm going to be really poorly. BUT Still all clean. Blood tests. Fine. What do I do ? They give me diazepam. "It's psychological". Bullcrap, but in my southern european country there is no medical protocol to help with vaccine side effects… I learn this after 5 to 6 hospital visits, for multiple different things after the vaccine. I keep thinking I might be having clotting because of this constant leg pain, so I take aspirin 100mg to thin the blood. When I learn about D-dimer tests, they are indeed high but only slightly. "Not at risk" they tell me.

What follows is a month of hell. Tremors in my hands, difficulty walking. Always weakness in the right side of my body. Headaches for 2 weeks. Can't work. I get these "flashes of lightning" on my nerves that cause immense pain when they happen. Alcohol seems to make it worse.

Eventually I find two things

The I-RECOVER protocol by the Front Line covid 19 care alliance

And this medium post on how to deal with Mast Cell Activation and derived serotonin dysfunction

Now I get the stuff from the medium post first. Seems to help, but slowly. My supplement diet is the one they suggest:

Nicotinic acid aka Niacin (most important and not nicotinamide or other forms) — 100mg
Vitamin C aka Ascorbic Acid — 1500mg
Vitamin D — 3000iu
Zinc — 15mg
Selenium — 50mcg
Quercetin — 500mg

I skip the Zinc and Selenium at first since my Vitamin D seems to come with it. I later re-add them since I actually think that my initial supplements were *weak*.

I think this is pretty essential - find a good brand and get everything from them. I was getting some white label Vitamin D that SAID it had Zinc and Selenium, but looking back it was cheapo stuff and probably didn't really work, as their effect was limited to none.

The Quercetin I initially have no idea what it is, so I don't get it. **Big mistake!**
As when I find the I-RECOVER protocol, and take the time to really read it, Quercetin is listed there also as a Mast Cell Stabilizer!

From the I-RECOVER protocol, I start realising (3 weeks in!) that some of the medication listed in it are plain anti histamines, sold over the counter! In fact I have some cetirizine at home!

**Week 3 I take cetirizine immediately and I am HUGELY RELIEVED of symptoms.** Pins and needles are gone, I stop having weird feelings around my arm, feeling like my hands weren't quite grabbing things the way they used to.

I'm blown away. What the hell is going on? I then read that histamines are both part of the immune system, and neurotransmitters! Neurotransmitters? And I'm having unexplainable neurological symptoms?

**Ok, I'm all in on the Mast Cell Activation Syndrome theory now. I read up on everything that's in the I-RECOVER protocol.**

And I find that the following medication is available in my country over the counter:

Type 1 Histamines - Cetrizine 10mg
Type 2 Histamines - Famotidine 20mg

Mast Cell Stabilizers - Rupatadine 10mg

Word to the wise - **the dosages listed on the FLCCC list appear excessive to me.** When I read up on the effect of excess anti histamines, it includes heart issues, so I'm careful and I never took the Famotidine. I only took the Cetrizine and Rupatidine once in the same day, and even then had weird heart palpitations. And I always did it within the allowed dosages - 10 mg, and never tripled down as it says on the I-RECOVER protocol. **The FLCCC has been accused of overdosing and I must agree, I believe their dosages are way too high unless you have medical supervision.**

**So here's my advice - take Cetrizine to see if your symptoms alleviate.**

If they do, **DO NOT MIX unless you feel it's absolutely safe and necessary. My impression is the type 1 and type 2 histamines are just symptom relief anyway.** Really you want to find the Rupatadine (or its replacement on the list, Ketotifen). And in my opinion, if **Cetrizine seems to relieve your symptoms a lot, take the Rupatadine only!** That should be the medicine you need to relieve your Mast Cell Activation, if that is what you have.

Since Cetrizine worked so well, I looked at the supplements recommended within the I-RECOVER Protocol and bought them all.

Here's my final supplement diet

- Vitamin C — 500mg twice daily
- Omega-3 Fatty Acids — 4gm/daily (Vascepa, Lovaza, or DHA/EPA)
- ~~Melatonin — 2–10mg nightly, start with low dose, increase as tolerated in absence of sleep disturbance.~~ I don't do this as I already have sleep trouble.
- Vitamin D3 — 2,000–4,000 IU daily
- Quercetin — 500mg
- NAC (N-acetyl Cisteine) 600mg
- L-Glutathione 50mg
- Zinc — 15mg - *included in my VitC*
- Selenium — 50mcg - *included in my VitC*

I add
- Cetirizine 10mg if I have symptoms, for symptom relief (ie like painkiller).
- Rupatadine 10mg, *but only when I have had no cetirizine that day to avoid heart issues*.

**Also started doing a low histamine diet and avoiding alcohol ENTIRELY, which is a pain.** However alcohol and histamine high foods definitely make it worse. In fact alcohol seems to be the #1 cause for any relapses, and even having half a beer this weekend caused some issues.

For a few days I also did take paracetamol, which seemed to help, before I got on with the cetirizine. However I was told by a doctor not to take it for too long (more than a couple of days) as it's hard on your liver.

My impression - the change after I started taking cetrizine was amazing. I had some STRONG pins and needles, like my nervous system was "rebooting", the first couple of days. After that, my motor, pain and sensitivity issues went away. I am now 1 month and 2 days in and mostly recovered.

**Unresolved - Curiously I have bipolar disorder and used to take medication in the morning (Lamictal) to avoid me becoming manic during the day. Now, I don't have to take it. Most likely if I didn't have bipolar I would've been in a depressive state.** This is for now unresolved but I'm wondering about getting my psychiatrist to get me Fluvoxamine, which is the recommended anti depressive by the FLCCC. So I might try that eventually and report back later. However right now I am actually taking less medication than before, so this is a bonus for me, while it might be that some folks get deep depression after the shot.

I hope this helps someone else.
